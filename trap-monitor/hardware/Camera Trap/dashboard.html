How We Compare — Per Competitor

Reconyx HyperFire 4K — $620 AUD

The research gold standard. 20-year reputation, 5-year warranty.
They win: 45m IR range (our Super Long module hits 30-35m), 4K video @ 30fps + audio, -20°C cold rating (irrelevant in tropics), 5-year warranty, trusted by ecologists worldwide
We win: Edge AI via Addax (they have none — 100% manual review), strobed IR (80% energy saving), 6 swappable IR modules, LTE built-in (theirs is $600+ add-on), no subscription, GPS anti-theft, stackable battery system, WiFi offload, $220 cheaper
Their weakness: No AI means researchers still manually review every image. At 1,000 images/day across 10 cameras, that's 40+ hours/week of sorting.

SpyPoint Flex G-36 — $155 AUD

Budget king. Dual-SIM LTE, solar option, massive market share.
They win: $244 cheaper (different market), dual-SIM LTE, 30m IR range (our Super Long module matches), huge dealer/retail network, solar built-in
We win: Edge AI via Addax (they have none), true 4K sensor (theirs is 36MP interpolated — actually ~4MP native), 940nm no-glow (theirs is 850nm visible glow), stackable battery (~107 days (214 with Extender) vs ~11 months), no subscription ($5+/mo adds up), Li-ion Solar backpack option, WiFi offload, GPS anti-theft, IP67 vs IP65

Browning Vision Pro Live — $341 AUD

Premium hunting cam with cloud AI and 1080p livestreaming.

Snap-On Backpacks

8-pin pogo connector (J_BP). Camera sees only battery voltage — doesn't know what's attached.
Standard12 AA NiMH caddy. No charging.Default~107 days
Field Station12 AA NiMH + trigger jack + LoRa slotPro~107 days
Li-ion SolarSealed Li-ion + BMS + MPPT solar + trigger jack + LoRa slotPremiumIndefinite
Extender12 AA NiMH spacer, pass-throughAdd-on+~107 days
Standard: Cheap, pre-loadable in the office. Snap-swap in 30 seconds in the field. The default for most deployments.
Field Station: Routes trigger output to weatherproof 2-pin jack. LoRa module slot for mesh relay. No solar — swap cells in the field like Standard.
Li-ion Solar: Sealed weatherproof unit with Li-ion pack, BMS, MPPT solar charger, trigger jack, and LoRa slot. Output regulated to match NiMH voltage range. "Set and forget" indefinite deployment. Eliminates wrong-battery-chemistry risk entirely.
Extender: Stacks between camera and Standard or Field Station backpack. Doubles field life. Pass-through for all 8 J_BP pins.

AI Platform — Addax

Open model architecture. The camera runs whatever INT8 .tflite model is on the SD card. Addax is the platform, not just one model.

Free Models

Addax publishes open models — AWC (135 Australian species, 95.2% accuracy, trained on 5M images from Parks Victoria). Load via SD card, costs nothing. More models added over time.

Custom Models

Pay Addax to train on your deployment data. A mine site needing specific threatened species for compliance. A ranger group targeting feral cats. A PhD student studying quolls. Model delivered as .tflite on SD card.

Community Models

Every deployment generates training data. Camera trap images feed back to Addax for retraining. Models get better with every camera in the field. Network effect — more cameras = better AI for everyone.
Behold has 2,000+ species but they're all North American/European. Zero Australian species. Our Addax partnership delivers the models that matter for each deployment region.

AI Trigger Platform — "The Camera Is the Brain"

We provide the intelligence. Third parties build the action. Researchers develop traps, AI models, and deployment strategies. The camera is the central core that makes it all work.

Trigger Pipeline

PIR wake → burst capture → AI classify → species confirmed → fire trigger output → alert → sleep. On-board TLP291 opto-coupler (3.75kV isolation) routed via Field Station backpack to weatherproof 2-pin jack.

Dual-Camera Confirmation

Two cameras at the same site, connected via LoRa mesh. Camera A detects feral cat → LoRa alert → Camera B confirms → either fires trigger. Reduces false positives for lethal control applications (net traps, bait dispensers).

Open Action Ecosystem

External devices connect via dry contact closure on the Field Station backpack jack. Net traps (HAND-style), bait dispensers, audio deterrents, gate actuators, sprinkler systems. Anyone can build an action device — we provide the AI trigger.
Configuration (via BLE): Target species/class filter, confidence threshold (default 0.70), pulse duration (default 2s), dual-camera mode (requires LoRa).
