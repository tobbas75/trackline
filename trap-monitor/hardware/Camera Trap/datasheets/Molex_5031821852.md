## Pin Configuration

**microSD standard pinout (8 signal pins + 2 card detect pins):**

| Pin | Name | Function | This Design |
|-----|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | DAT2 | Data line 2 | SDMMC2_D2 |
| 2 | CD/DAT3 | Card detect / Data line 3 | SDMMC2_D3 (directly, or via pull-up) |
| 3 | CMD | Command | SDMMC2_CMD |
| 4 | VDD | Supply voltage (3.3V) | 3.3V rail via ferrite bead |
| 5 | CLK | Clock | SDMMC2_CK |
| 6 | VSS | Ground | GND |
| 7 | DAT0 | Data line 0 | SDMMC2_D0 |
| 8 | DAT1 | Data line 1 | SDMMC2_D1 |
| CD1 | Card Detect 1 | Card detect switch (common) | GND or GPIO input |
| CD2 | Card Detect 2 | Card detect switch (NO contact) | GPIO input with pull-up |

**Card detect switch operation:**
- **Card inserted:** CD1 and CD2 are shorted (switch closed)
- **Card removed:** CD1 and CD2 are open (switch open)

Typical connection: CD1 to GND, CD2 to GPIO with external pull-up (10-100 kohm to 3.3V). Card inserted = GPIO LOW. Card removed = GPIO HIGH.

**VERIFY card detect pin assignments and switch polarity from the Molex mechanical drawing. Some connectors swap the normally-open/normally-closed logic.**

## Footprint / PCB Layout

**VERIFY all dimensions from the Molex drawing 987652-0671 before PCB layout. Values below are approximate from distributor listings.**

Approximate footprint dimensions:
- Signal pads: 8 pads at 1.10 mm pitch
- Card detect pads: 2 pads (separate from signal row)
- Mounting/shield tabs: 4 solder tabs for mechanical retention
- Overall footprint: ~16 x 16 mm (including tabs)

**Recommended layout practices:**
1. Follow Molex recommended pad dimensions exactly from the drawing
2. Place mounting tabs on solid copper pour for mechanical strength
3. Route signal traces away from mounting tab pads (ESD path)
4. Add ground guard ring around connector for EMI
5. Place 100 nF + 10 uF bypass on VDD pin within 5 mm of connector

**Card clearance:** Ensure no components within the card insertion/ejection path. The push-push mechanism extends the card ~2 mm beyond the connector body during ejection.

## Power

| State | Current | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| SD card idle | ~0.1-0.5 mA | Depends on card (varies widely) |
| SD card active (write) | 50-200 mA | Burst during image write |
| SD card sleep | ~0.01-0.1 mA | Card in sleep/standby mode |
| Connector (no card) | 0 | No quiescent draw |

The SD card power consumption varies enormously between card manufacturers and speed classes. Budget 200 mA peak for writes. The 3.3V rail (AP63300 U11, 3A) has ample headroom.

**Power gating:** The SD card is NOT power-gated in this design — it shares the always-on 3.3V rail. Card power management is done via SDIO commands (CMD7 deselect, CMD15 go inactive).

## Application Notes for This Design

1. **4-bit SDIO.** Using SDMMC2 4-bit mode for maximum throughput. Each 4K JPEG frame is ~1-2 MB. Burst of 10 frames = 10-20 MB written in <2 seconds at 4-bit 25 MHz.

2. **Card capacity.** Design targets 128 GB or 256 GB microSD cards. At ~25 MB per burst (10 frames + metadata), 256 GB holds ~10,000 bursts.

3. **Write endurance.** Industrial/endurance-rated microSD cards are recommended for field deployment. Consumer cards may fail after heavy write cycling in hot environments.

4. **Card detect.** Use card detect GPIO to prevent SDIO commands when no card is present. Log card insertion/removal events.

5. **ESD protection.** The connector's stainless steel shell provides some shielding. Add TVS diodes on DAT0-3, CMD, CLK if ESD testing fails. The USBLC6-2SC6 (U_USB in BOM) could be replicated for SD lines if needed.

6. **Enclosure sealing.** The microSD slot must be accessible for card swaps but sealed against water/dust when closed. The IP67 enclosure design needs a gasketed card access door.

7. **Hot-swap.** The push-push mechanism supports hot-swap. Firmware should detect card removal via card detect pin and gracefully close any open files before the card is physically removed.

## Known Issues

1. **Profile height variation.** Different sources report 1.45 mm or 1.55 mm height. This matters for enclosure clearance. VERIFY from Molex mechanical drawing.

2. **Card detect reliability.** Mechanical card detect switches can wear or corrode in humid environments. Consider firmware-based detection (try SDIO CMD0 and check response) as a backup.

3. **Anti-card fly-out.** The push-push mechanism includes a retention feature, but extreme vibration (transport, animal impact) could still eject the card. The enclosure card access door provides secondary retention.

4. **Operating temperature.** Rated -25C to +85C. The lower limit (-25C) is above the sensor and MCU ratings (-40C). In tropical Australia this is not a concern (minimum expected ~5C), but for cold-climate deployments this connector would be the limiting component.

5. **Gold contact wear.** Gold plating on contacts wears over repeated insertions. 10,000 cycle rating is adequate for weekly card swaps over the product lifetime, but avoid unnecessary card removal during development.
