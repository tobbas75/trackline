## Pin Configuration — SMA (DO-214AC)

```
   Cathode band
   ┌──────────┐
   │  ██████  │  ← Band marks CATHODE
   │          │
   │   SS34   │
   │          │
   └──────────┘
   Anode (bottom pad)
```

| Terminal | Location | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Anode | Bottom pad (unmarked end) | Current flows IN here |
| Cathode | Top pad (band-marked end) | Current flows OUT here, towards load |

**In the 6S2P protection circuit:** Anode connects to the positive terminal of each NiMH string. Cathode connects to the common positive bus (battery output). This allows current to flow FROM the battery string TO the load, but blocks reverse current from one string charging the other.

## Power — Forward Voltage Drop Impact

### Battery Voltage Range with SS34 Diodes

| Battery State | Voltage (no diode) | Voltage (with SS34) | Notes |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Full charge (6 x 1.4V) | 8.4V | ~8.1V | Vf ~0.3V at low current |
| Nominal (6 x 1.2V) | 7.2V | ~6.9V | Vf ~0.3V |
| Discharged (6 x 1.0V) | 6.0V | ~5.7V | Vf ~0.3V at low current |
| Under load (1A burst) | ~7.5V | ~7.15V | Vf ~0.35V at 1A |
| Under heavy load (2A) | ~7.0V | ~6.5V | Vf ~0.5V at 2-3A combined |

### Impact on AP63300 Headroom

AP63300 minimum input = 3.8V. Worst case with SS34:
- Discharged battery + diode drop: 5.7V
- Headroom: 5.7V - 3.8V = 1.9V
- **Safe.** Even with internal resistance drops under load, there is ample margin.

### Usable Capacity Impact

The 0.3V diode drop effectively removes ~0.3V from the top and bottom of the usable voltage range:
- Without diodes: 6.0V to 8.4V (full range)
- With diodes: 5.7V to 8.1V (shifted down by Vf)

The firmware battery voltage thresholds must account for this:
- BATT_ADC reads the voltage AFTER the diode (at the AP63300 input)
- Low battery threshold should be set based on the post-diode voltage
- The diode drop means ~300mAh of capacity at the bottom of the discharge curve is lost (the battery reaches the cutoff voltage sooner because the system sees Vbatt - Vf)

**Estimated capacity loss:** ~300 mAh out of 4000 mAh = 7.5% = ~8 days of battery life. This is why the POWER_BUDGET.md shows 99 days with Schottky diodes vs 107 days without.

### Power Dissipation in Diodes

| Load | Vf | Power per diode | Notes |
|------|-----|-----------------|-------|
| 50 uA (sleep) | ~0.20V | 0.01 mW | Negligible |
| 100 mA | ~0.30V | 30 mW | Light active load |
| 500 mA | ~0.35V | 175 mW | Moderate load |
| 1 A | ~0.35V | 350 mW | Heavy load |
| 2 A (combined strings) | ~0.45V | 450 mW per diode | Near-peak load |
| 3 A (absolute peak) | ~0.50V | 750 mW per diode | Brief transients only |

At 2A combined load (each string contributes ~1A), each diode dissipates ~350mW. With theta-JA ~75 C/W: deltaT = 26C. At 52C ambient: Tj = 78C. Well within limits.

## Application Notes for This Design

### 6S2P Parallel String Protection

```
String 1 (6S, 2000mAh)           String 2 (6S, 2000mAh)
    (+)                               (+)
     │                                 │
   [D_SCHK1 - SS34]               [D_SCHK2 - SS34]
     │            Cathodes joined      │
     └──────────────┬──────────────────┘
                    │
              Common VBATT+
                    │
              To AP63300 inputs
```

**Purpose:** Prevents reverse current between parallel NiMH strings if one string has lower voltage (deeper discharge, weaker cells, or failed cell). Without protection, the higher-voltage string would charge the lower-voltage string, potentially reversing a cell and causing damage or hydrogen gas buildup.

**Why Schottky (not silicon)?** Lower forward voltage (~0.3V vs ~0.7V for 1N5401). The 0.3V drop is the penalty for protection — silicon diodes would waste 0.7V = double the capacity loss.

**Phase 1 vs Production:**
- Phase 1: SS34 diodes installed. Accept the 0.3V / 300mAh capacity penalty for safety with untested cell batches.
- Production: May remove diodes if cells are matched at assembly (< 50mV difference between strings) and firmware implements per-string voltage monitoring. Recovers ~8 days battery life.

### Thermal Considerations

In a sealed IP67 enclosure at 52C ambient:
- Sleep current (~50uA) through diode: power = 0.01mW. No thermal impact.
- Active peak (~2A for 3 seconds): power = ~450mW per diode for 3 seconds. Thermal mass of SMA package absorbs this without significant temperature rise.
- SMA package handles 3A continuous at 25C ambient. At 52C ambient, derate to ~2.5A continuous — still above our peak draw.

### Reverse Leakage

At 8.4V reverse bias (one string full, the other disconnected):
- Ir at 25C: ~50 uA max (likely much less at 8.4V vs 40V rated)
- Ir at 52C: ~100-200 uA (increases with temperature)

This leakage is from the battery string through the reverse-biased diode. At ~100uA, it would drain the battery at ~100uA — comparable to the system sleep current. However, this scenario only occurs if one string is disconnected (not in normal operation). During normal operation, both diodes are forward biased and there is no reverse leakage concern.

**If reverse leakage at elevated temperature is a concern,** monitor the actual leakage current on the prototype at 50-60C.

## Known Issues / Errata

1. **Vf increases with temperature.** At 125C, Vf at 3A may be slightly higher than 25C values. However, the reverse leakage current also increases significantly with temperature. Net effect at 52C ambient is minimal — Vf drops slightly with temperature (Schottky positive temp coefficient of Vf is lower than silicon).

   **Correction:** Schottky diodes actually have a NEGATIVE temperature coefficient for Vf (Vf decreases with temperature) but a POSITIVE coefficient for leakage current (Ir increases dramatically). At moderate temperatures (50-60C), the net effect is beneficial for forward voltage but watch for increased reverse leakage. VERIFY from the specific manufacturer's datasheet.

2. **No manufacturer standardization.** "SS34" is not a JEDEC standard part number. Different manufacturers may have slightly different specs. Verify Vf and Ir from the specific part received. For production, specify a manufacturer (e.g., Vishay SS34-E3) to ensure consistency.

3. **SMA package orientation.** The cathode band is on one end of the SMA package. Ensure the PCB silkscreen marks the cathode correctly. Reversed diodes will conduct in the wrong direction and defeat the protection function — worse, they would dead-short the battery strings together through the body diodes of both reversed parts.

4. **Phase 1 decision.** These diodes are explicitly a Phase 1 safety measure. The 7.5% capacity penalty (~8 days battery life) is significant. For production, cell matching and firmware cutoff should replace the diodes. Track this as a Phase 2 optimization.

5. **No protection against single-cell failure.** The Schottky diodes protect against inter-string reverse current but do NOT protect against a single cell reversing within a string. If one cell in a 6S string fails open, the string voltage drops and the other string takes the full load — the diode for the healthy string handles the full current (up to 2-3A, within its 3A rating). If one cell fails short, that string's voltage drops by 1.2V but continues to operate. These failure modes are inherent to series NiMH and are not mitigated by the Schottky diodes.
