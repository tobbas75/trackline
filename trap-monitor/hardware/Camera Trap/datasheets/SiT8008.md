## Power

- VDD = 1.8V, 24 MHz output, 15 pF load
- Typical current: 3.6 mA
- Power: 3.6 mA Г— 1.8V = **6.5 mW typical**

### Standby Mode (OE = LOW)
- Oscillator core stops, output goes Hi-Z
- Typical current: 1.2 ВөA
- Power: 1.2 ВөA Г— 1.8V = **2.2 ВөW typical**

### Power-Gated (VDD removed)
- In this design, the oscillator is powered from the 1.8V sensor rail (TPS7A02, U12)
- When sensor is not in use, the entire 1.8V rail can be disabled
- Zero current draw when power-gated

## Application Notes for This Design

### Connection
- OUT (pin 5) вҶ’ IMX678 INCK pin (direct connection, no series resistor needed)
- VDD (pin 4) вҶ’ 1.8V sensor rail (TPS7A02 U12 output) with 100 nF bypass cap
- OE (pin 1) вҶ’ Can be left floating (internal pull-up, always enabled) or connected to a GPIO for explicit enable control. In this design, power-gating the 1.8V rail serves the same purpose.
- GND (pin 2) вҶ’ Ground plane

### Layout
- Place within 10 mm of the IMX678 INCK pin
- Route OUT trace as controlled impedance is not necessary at 24 MHz вҖ” keep trace short and avoid running parallel to high-speed digital signals
- 100 nF bypass cap directly at VDD/GND pins (0402, within 2 mm)
- Ground pad under the package connects to GND (if applicable вҖ” SOT-23-5 has no exposed pad)

### Power Sequencing
- The oscillator powers up with the 1.8V rail (same as IMX678 DOVDD)
- Startup time <5 ms вҖ” the IMX678 XCLR release delay (500 ms) provides ample margin for oscillator startup
- No separate enable sequencing required

## Known Issues / Errata

1. **Part number decode discrepancy.** The BOM notes "Вұ25ppm" but the part number suffix "G" likely indicates Вұ50 ppm. If Вұ25 ppm is genuinely required (it is not вҖ” IMX678 PLL is very tolerant), the stability suffix needs to change to "F" (Вұ25 ppm) or similar. For this application, Вұ50 ppm is more than adequate. **Resolve before ordering.**

2. **1.8V variant stock.** The 1.8V, 24 MHz, SOT-23-5 combination is standard but may not be stocked at all distributors. Check DigiKey/Mouser stock before assuming availability. The 3.3V variant is more commonly stocked but would not be compatible with the 1.8V DOVDD rail.

3. **OE pin behaviour.** The "E" suffix means OE is active HIGH. If the design needs active LOW enable, the suffix would be "D" instead. Current design uses power-gating rather than OE control, so this is not critical.

4. **No exposed thermal pad.** SOT-23-5 has no thermal pad. Thermal dissipation is through the pins only. At 6.5 mW, this is not a concern.
