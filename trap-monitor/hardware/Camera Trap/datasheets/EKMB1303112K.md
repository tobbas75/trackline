## Output Circuit — CRITICAL DESIGN NOTE

**This is ACTIVE HIGH with a pull-DOWN resistor. This is NOT the typical open-drain-to-ground with pull-UP that most engineers assume.**

### How the output works

```
         VDD (2.3-4.0V)
          │
          ├──── Internal P-FET (source)
          │
          ├──── Pin 2 (OUT / Drain)
          │
          ├──── External 47k+ pull-DOWN to GND
          │
         GND
```

- **Idle (no detection):** Internal FET is OFF. Pin 2 floats (high impedance). External pull-down resistor pulls output LOW.
- **Detection:** Internal FET turns ON, sourcing current from VDD through FET to Pin 2. Output goes HIGH (approximately VDD minus FET Vds).
- **Output returns LOW** after detection pulse ends (~2 s) and FET turns OFF again.

### External pull-down resistor selection

The pull-down resistor must be chosen so that output current does not exceed 100 uA:

```
R_pulldown >= VDD / I_out_max
R_pulldown >= 4.0V / 100 uA = 40 kohm (minimum)
```

**Use 47 kohm or higher.** In this design: 47 kohm to GND (as specified in BOM.csv).

### Why this matters

Most PIR sensors (and most open-drain outputs generally) use an N-FET that sinks to GND, requiring a pull-UP resistor and producing an ACTIVE LOW output. The Panasonic EKMB does the opposite. If you wire it with a pull-up (as instinct suggests for "open drain"), you get VDD on the output pin at all times and never see a detection edge.

**config.h cross-reference:** PIR signals connect to STM32U0 (U0_PA0 for PIR_1, U0_PA1 for PIR_2). The U0 aggregates PIR events and wakes the STM32N6 via PIR_WAKE_FROM_U0_PIN (PG3). U0 firmware must configure EXTI on rising edge for ACTIVE HIGH.

## Detection Zone Pattern

The EKMB1303112K uses a black polyethylene Fresnel lens that creates a multi-zone detection pattern. The "1303" long-distance type has a narrower, deeper detection cone optimised for long range (12 m) rather than wide coverage.

**Zone layout (approximate — VERIFY from Panasonic catalog zone diagram):**

- Multiple detection zones arranged in a roughly conical pattern
- Horizontal coverage: ~94 deg total
- Vertical coverage: ~82 deg total
- Zone density is higher at centre, sparser at edges
- Each zone is a sector where the Fresnel lens focuses thermal IR onto the pyroelectric element
- Movement between zones (not within a single zone) triggers detection

**Dual-PIR configuration in this design:** Two EKMB1303112K sensors are angled to provide overlapping coverage across the camera field of view. Firmware on the STM32U0 OR-gates both PIR outputs — either sensor triggers a capture burst.

## Power

| State | Current | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Standby (sensing) | 1 uA | Dominant sleep budget component is elsewhere |
| During detection pulse | ~100 uA max | Through pull-down resistor, brief |

At 3.3V supply with 47 kohm pull-down:
- Standby: 1 uA (sensor) + ~0 uA (pull-down, output LOW/float)
- Detection pulse: 1 uA (sensor) + ~70 uA (3.3V / 47k through pull-down) = ~71 uA for ~2 s

**Total daily contribution (70 triggers/day, ~2 s each):** ~140 s at ~71 uA = ~0.003 mAh — negligible.

## Application Notes for This Design

1. **Supply voltage:** Fed from 3.3V rail (always-on AP63300 U11). Within 2.3-4.0V operating range.

2. **Output connection:** Pin 2 (OUT) connects to STM32U0 GPIO with 47 kohm pull-down to GND. EXTI configured for **rising edge** (active HIGH).

3. **Warm-up blanking:** After power-up, ignore PIR outputs for 30 seconds. The spec says 10 s minimum, but thermal stability improves significantly at 30 s. Firmware on U0 implements this blanking window.

4. **PIR lockout:** config.h defines PIR_LOCKOUT_S = 5 seconds between triggers. This prevents rapid re-triggering from the same animal.

5. **Temperature concern:** Operating range is -20C to +60C. The white/light enclosure is critical — black enclosures reach 70C+ in tropical sun, which exceeds the sensor's rating. With white IP67 polycarbonate enclosure, internal temperature should stay within spec.

6. **Lens orientation:** The long-axis of the Fresnel lens pattern should be aligned with the expected animal travel direction for maximum sensitivity. In this design, both PIRs are oriented to cover the camera's field of view.

7. **Mounting:** Through-hole TO-5 package. Solder directly to main board with lens protruding through enclosure front panel. Seal with silicone gasket around lens.

## Known Issues

1. **Output polarity confusion.** The single most common mistake with this sensor. Document prominently in schematic, BOM, and review guide. The BOM.csv already notes "ACTIVE HIGH output (pull-DOWN 47k to GND, NOT pull-up)".

2. **60C upper temperature limit.** Marginal for tropical deployment. Enclosure colour and ventilation are critical. Monitor field temperature data from first deployments.

3. **No sensitivity adjustment.** The EKMB series has fixed sensitivity set by the internal signal processing. Cannot be tuned in firmware. If detection range needs changing, a different lens variant (different part number suffix) is required.

4. **Blanking period after detection.** The sensor has an internal blanking period (~2 s) after each detection. Combined with firmware lockout (5 s), effective retrigger time is ~5 s minimum.

5. **False triggers from thermal transients.** Rapid temperature changes (cloud shadow, rain splash) can cause false triggers. The dual-PIR AND/OR logic in firmware can help filter these — currently OR-gated (either PIR triggers), but could be changed to AND-gated (both PIRs must trigger) for noisy environments at the cost of detection coverage.
