### CTR Rank Designations

The TLP291 is available in multiple CTR (Current Transfer Ratio) ranks:

| Rank | CTR Range | Confidence |
|------|-----------|------------|
| BL | 50-100% | [TRAINING-DATA] verify |
| YR | 100-200% | [TRAINING-DATA] verify |
| **GR** | **200-600%** | [TRAINING-DATA] verify |
| TP | 100-600% (wide range) | [TRAINING-DATA] verify |

**This design uses GR rank** (200-600% CTR). The high CTR allows low LED drive current while still saturating the phototransistor output, which is important for minimizing GPIO current draw from the STM32N6.

### Suffix Decode

`TLP291(GR-TP,SE)`:
- **GR** = CTR rank (200-600%)
- **TP** = Tape and reel packaging
- **SE** = Lead-free / RoHS compliant

## Application Notes for This Design

### Trigger Output Isolation

The TLP291 (U_TRIG) provides galvanic isolation between the camera trap MCU and the external trigger output. This isolates the field cable (which may connect to traps, bait stations, or deterrent devices) from the camera's internal electronics.

**Circuit (from DESIGN_SPEC.md / BOM.csv):**

```
STM32N6                    TLP291                 Backpack Connector
                                                  J_BP pins 3-4
PG6 ──[R_LED]──► Anode (1)    Collector (4) ──── Pin 3 (TRIG+)
                  │                │
                 GND ◄── Cathode (2)   Emitter (3) ──── Pin 4 (TRIG-)
```

- **PG6** drives the LED side via a current-limiting resistor
- **Collector/Emitter** connect to the backpack connector pins 3 and 4
- The external device provides its own pull-up or supply on the trigger lines
- **>3.75 kV isolation** protects the camera from field cable faults, lightning transients, and ground loops

### LED Drive Current Calculation

With GR rank (CTR 200-600% min) at IF = 5 mA:
- IC (output) = IF x CTR = 5 mA x 2.0 = 10 mA minimum
- More than sufficient to drive most trigger inputs

For the LED resistor (R_LED), assuming PG6 = 3.3V output, VF = 1.2V, IF = 5 mA:
- R_LED = (3.3V - 1.2V) / 5 mA = 420 ohm (nearest E24: 390 ohm or 470 ohm)

**Verify the actual R_LED value in the schematic.** The BOM does not specify the exact value for the optocoupler LED resistor.

### Trigger Input Sense

A separate path (R_TRIG_SENSE = 100k ohm + D_TRIG_TVS 3.3V bidirectional TVS) connects J_BP pin 3 to STM32U0 PA3 (TRIG_SENSE). This allows the camera to sense whether an external trigger device is connected and its state, independently of the optocoupler output drive.

### Response Time

Rise/fall times of 3/4 us are more than adequate for trigger output applications. The trigger pulse duration is typically 100 ms or longer.

## Known Issues / Errata

1. **CTR degrades with temperature and age.** At high temperature (+85 degC) and after extended use, CTR can drop to 50% of room-temperature value. The GR rank (200-600%) provides adequate margin, but if operating near the temperature limit, verify minimum CTR at worst-case temperature.

2. **CTR varies with IF.** The rated CTR (200-600%) is specified at IF = 5 mA. At lower drive currents (e.g., 1 mA), CTR may be significantly lower. Design the LED drive resistor for at least 5 mA.

3. **LED resistor value.** The exact R_LED value is not specified in the BOM — it is listed under "R_various" passives. The schematic must define this resistor based on the GPIO output voltage and desired IF.

4. **Phototransistor saturation.** For switching applications, ensure VCE_sat < logic LOW threshold of the receiving device. At IF=5mA and CTR=200%, IC=10mA with VCE_sat ~0.2V — this is well below any standard logic threshold.

5. **Isolation rating.** The 3750 Vrms isolation is for 1 minute per UL/IEC. For continuous isolation at lower voltages (typical field cable voltages are < 48V), this is more than adequate. The primary risk is lightning-induced transients on long field cable runs, which the optocoupler + TVS diode combination addresses.
