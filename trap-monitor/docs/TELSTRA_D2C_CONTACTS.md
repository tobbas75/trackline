## Starlink Direct to Cell

### Business Portal

- **Global:** https://starlink.com/business/direct-to-cell
- **Australia:** https://starlink.com/au/business/direct-to-cell
- **Direct portal:** https://direct.starlink.com/
- **Purpose:** Register interest as an IoT device maker, partner enquiries

### D2C Compatibility

- Compatible with off-the-shelf **CAT-1, CAT-1bis, and CAT-4 modems**
- Must be **3GPP Release 10+** compliant
- Must support **existing bands of operation** in the target country
- Our EG800Q-EU (CAT-1bis, Band 7 + Band 28) fits these requirements

## D2C Service Status in Australia

| Milestone | Date | Detail |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Partnership announced | January 2025 | Telstra + SpaceX Starlink collaboration |
| Trials | April–May 2025 | 55,000+ test messages across NT, WA, QLD, NSW |
| Commercial launch (SMS) | June 2025 | Samsung Galaxy S25 only, selected plans |
| IoT device support | TBD | Listed as "upcoming feature" — not formally open yet |
| Voice/data D2C | Expected 2026 | Depends on SpaceX Starship heavier satellite payloads |
| D2C satellites launched | January 2026 | 650+ DTC satellites in constellation |

### Coverage

- Covers all of mainland Australia (except AU Radio Quiet Zone in WA and remote offshore territories)
- Telstra terrestrial covers 3M km² (99.7% population) — but AU is 7.6M km², so >50% of land has no terrestrial signal
- D2C fills the gap for the other 4.6M km²

1. **Email M2M hardware team** — introduce the trap monitor, mention D2C on Band 7, ask about certification pathway and whether D2C is open to IoT devices

3. **Contact M2M One** — may facilitate Telstra introductions as an existing IoT partner
4. **Telstra Enterprise IoT web form** — broader enquiry that may route to business development
